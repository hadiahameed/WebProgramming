That Time I Made My First Dish

My first solo cooking venture was making Baingan ka Bharta (Scrambled eggplant) (Thanks to YouTube/Kabita's kitchen). In this post I will share a few takeaways from my experience.

Fennel seeds (saunf) looks just like cumin seeds (zeera) so don't add fennel seeds by mistake unless you want your dish to taste like Paan.

Don't let "Why doesn't my dish look as good as the youtube lady's?" bring you down. Keep cookin'! You're crushin' it! If it doesn't taste fun, add a little ketchup but don't tell your roommate (or any cooking expert) that you did that. (Hehe). Also, if you are a novice chef, keep Burnol/bandage close at hand because you are bound to burn/cut yourself several times in the course of your (mis)adventure. Moreover, always buy a non-stick handi no matter how much the sweet, Indian aunty tells you that the steel pan would work. It won't.

Finally, eat it with relish and gratitude however it tastes/looks. Happy cooking!
